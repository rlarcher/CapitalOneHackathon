Lesson: And/Or


You'll often hear people talk about doing something and something else. You might also hear people do something or something else. Bryce wants to see a movie and get dinner. But Aleka is going to invite Noam or Parag to her party. We use these words a lot, but we don't always think about what they mean. Even more interestingly, "or" can mean one thing in conversational English and something completely different in Computer Science!

The differences between "and" and "or" aren;t immediately obvious, but we're going to meet two kids who love Legos can help us figure out the difference.

Priya is flexible and likes building with blue or red Legos. This means:
1. If you give Priya a bag of only blue Legos, she?ll be happy
2. If you give Priya a bag of only red Legos, she?ll be happy
3. If you give Priya a mixed bag that contains blue and red Lego pieces, she?ll be happy
4. But, if you give Priya Legos that are green, yellow, or any other color, she won?t be happy

Let's say x represents a Lego, B represents blue, and R represents red. To show that Priya likes blue or red Legos, you can write:
x = B || x = R
where || means or.
Franklin is less flexible than Priya because he only likes blue and red Legos. This means:
1. If you give Franklin a bag of only blue Legos, he'll be unhappy
2. If you give Franklin a bag of only red Legos, he'll be unhappy
3. If you give Franklin a mixed bag that contains blue and red Lego pieces, he'll be happy
4. But, if you give Franklin Legos that are green, yellow, or any other color, he'll be unhappy
Let's say x represents a Lego, B represents blue, and R represents red. To show that Priya likes blue or red Legos, you can write:
x = B && x = R
where && means and.

So, if Aleka is going to invite Noam or Parag to her party, this means that she wants at least one of the two guys at her party. She's okay with:
1. Inviting Noam, but not Parag
2. Inviting Parag, but not Noam
3. Inviting Parag and inviting Noam

Meanwhile, Bryce wants to see a movie and get dinner. That means that he wants to do both and won't be happy if you skip the movie, skip dinner, or skip both.
