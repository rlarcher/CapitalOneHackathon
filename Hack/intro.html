Welcome to Code:Kids

A child’s mind can grasp virtually any idea, if it’s explained to them the right way.

That’s why the teacher is an important bridge between the student and a practically infinite world of knowledge.

There is a wealth of coding forums and tutorials online. But when that knowledge is bogged down in botched syntax and abstractly-worded explanations, it seems virtually impossible to piece together a meaningful, coherent idea of what coding does and why it matters.

Until now.

As computer science students, we realize that it is time for a new approach to teaching kids to code. Instead of getting weighed down by semicolons and parentheses, we focus on what’s really important: the concepts behind computer science. We walk young learners through the ideas of for loops or if-else constructs by applying them to relevant, easy-to-grasp situations like making breakfast or going to the movies.

Our mission is to introduce kids to coding in a way that will stick and will give them a big head start with their computer science education.

It’s time to change the way we think about computer science education. And that’s what Code:Kids is all about.
